# engineering drawings

***

## types of lines (in order of precedence):
* visible lines
    * continuous and thick (0.5 - 0.6 mm) line is used to represent all visible edges and boundaries. 
* hidden lines
    * a dashed and medium thick (0.35 - 0.45 mm) line is used to indicate hidden edges and boundaries that cannot be seen.
* centre lines
    * a repeating long dash followed by a short dash and thin (0.3 mm) line is used to represent axes of symmetry for any  circular, cylindrical, or spherical shapes.
    * all dimensions are in millimetres (not metres)
    * scales are always written as drawing : actual
    * it is essential to know how to convert both from isometric to orthographic and from orthographic to isometric
