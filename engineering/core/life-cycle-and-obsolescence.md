# life cycle and obsolescence

***

## LCA
* LCA provides a rigorous analysis of the environmental impacts of all stages of the production process.
#### stages of the life cycle:
1. materials acquisition
    * involves obtaining raw materials necessary to produce the engineered product.
2. processing materials
    * some materials require processing, refining, or transformation to obtain the necessary components.
3. manufacturing
    * involves the actual production of the engineered product, where components are assembled and integrated.
4. packaging
5. transportation
6. maintenance/operation
7. reuse/recycle/disposal

## obsolescence
* psychological obsolescence
    * cause people to feel ashamed for having an old product, and build hype for the new one.
* technical obsolescence
    * driven by advancements in technology, rendering older versions less compatible
* functional obsolescence
    * results from utility or performance issues making a product less effective or desirable
* planned obsolescence
    * deliberate strategy by manufacturers to design products with a limited lifespan, encouraging frequent replacements or upgrades.
